Components

What exactly is interpersonal effectiveness? People who are interpersonally effective are successful in a wide range of interactions with others. They are able to influence others and persuade them to cooperate. They are able to defuse conflict when it arises. They are able to offer support when others are distressed, and they are able to ask for and receive support when they need it. Overall, people who are interpersonally effective connect with other people in important ways and gain the respect of others. In essence, interpersonal effectiveness is the ability to understand the world around you and then to respond in a way that promotes your social and personal success.

Throughout this course, you will be introduced to concepts and skills that will help you become more confident and competent in your personal, professional, and academic interactions.

To do this, you need to understand the four basic components of interpersonal effectiveness:

  • Competence
  • Emotional intelligence
  • Mindfulness
  • Ethics
