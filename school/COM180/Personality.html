Personality

“Personality” is a word that we hear often. But do we truly understand what it means?

Our personalities are made up of those persistent and enduring behavior patterns that we express in a wide variety of situations. For instance, when Susan gets angry, she tends to get loud and excitable. This is not a one-time event; Susan’s coworkers, family, and friends have seen this behavior at work, at home, and in various social settings. That pattern is one thread of Susan’s personality.

Eight personality factors have a substantial impact on interpersonal relations and job performance. The interpretations and meanings of these factors provide useful information because they help you pinpoint important areas for personal development and improvement. Although these factors are partially inherited from our parents, most people can improve them over time with conscious effort.
