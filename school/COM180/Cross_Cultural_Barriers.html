Cross-Cultural Barriers

Cultural differences can create barriers. To be interpersonally effective with your classmates and coworkers, you need to understand some fundamental guidelines for overcoming cultural barriers.

  1. Be sensitive to the fact that cross-cultural barriers exist in your class and in your workplace.
  2. Show respect for all classmates and co-workers.
  3. Use straightforward language when writing or speaking. Minimize the use of idioms and analogies. For example, if you were from a different culture, what would you think if someone wrote, “He’s on the wagon”?
  4. Observe cultural differences in etiquette. In some countries, it is important to address superiors by their last name, whereas in other countries, it is acceptable to address superiors on a first-name basis.
  5. Be sensitive to differences in nonverbal communication. For example, in some cultures, a woman is expected to extend her hand first to shake with a man.
  6. Do not be diverted by style, accent, grammar, or personal appearance. A brilliant individual from another culture may still be learning your language.
  7. Be attentive to individual differences in appearance. Pay more attention to someone’s distinguishing features, such as their eye color or presence of a dimple, and less attention to features that are associated with race, such as skin color.
  8. Correctly pronounce the names of people from other countries with whom you interact.
