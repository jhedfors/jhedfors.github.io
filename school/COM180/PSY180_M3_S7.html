Cultural Values

One way to understand how cultures differ is to look at their values. We will examine nine cultural dimensions:

  • Performance orientation is the degree to which people are rewarded for performance and excellence.
  • Assertiveness is the degree to which individuals are assertive, confrontational, and aggressive in their relationships with one another.
  • Time orientation is the value that individuals attach to time when meeting deadlines, attending meetings, and doing business.
  • Humane orientation is the degree to which individuals are rewarded for being kind, fair, and caring toward each other.
  • In-group collectivism is the degree to which individuals express pride, loyalty, and cohesiveness.
  • Gender egalitarianism is the degree to which a culture minimizes gender inequality.
  • Acceptance of power and authority is the degree to which members of a society expect power to be distributed unequally.
  • Work orientation is the number of hours per week or year that people expect to invest in work versus leisure.
  • Social support seeking is the degree to which people seek out others to help them with difficult problems by listening, offering sympathy, and giving advice.
