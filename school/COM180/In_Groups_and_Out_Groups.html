In-Groups and Out-Groups

People of various cultures tend to form two types of groups:

  • In-groups
  • Out-groups

In-groups consist of the people with whom we identify ourselves. These people make us feel comfortable and accepted. If you are the member of an in-group, you have an insider’s perspective. This means that you have exclusive knowledge of the language and values of your group. Can you think of an example of where you have in-group status (at work, in your family, with friends, as a student)?

Belonging to in-groups contributes to every person’s social identity and adds to the self-concept that is based on membership in these groups.

Out-groups are the groups that we view as different from us. If you are part of an out-group, you have an outsider’s perspective. You may understand some concepts about the culture, but you will never truly be part of the group.
