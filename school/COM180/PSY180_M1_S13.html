Setting SMART Goals

Now that you have read about SMART goals, let’s walk through setting a SMART goal. Click here for a SMART goal example you can print out for reference.

Your friend Amy is a 30-year-old single mom of two kids. She recently came to you complaining about being frustrated with several areas of her life. She feels like others are getting promoted in her workplace and she is not being considered. She has been taking online classes, but because of her many different responsibilities, she has not been doing well in class. And she feels out of shape and would like to lose some weight. You challenge her to take one of those aspects and create a SMART goal.
