Competence

“Competence” describes your ability to accomplish your goals. A broad concept that is addressed in modules throughout this course, competence is comprised of two elements:

  • Appropriateness
  • Effectiveness

“Appropriateness” describes your ability to fulfill the norms, expectations, and rules in a given situation. For example, on a first date, it would not be appropriate to start discussing wedding plans or future children.

“Effectiveness” describes your ability to accomplish your goals.

Ideally, competence involves being able to accomplish your goals, while fulfilling the norms and expectations of the situation and maintaining your relationships. However, competence is not a black-and-white concept. Nobody demonstrates completely competent behavior all of the time. Competence varies across a spectrum of appropriateness and effectiveness and depends on the individual circumstance.
