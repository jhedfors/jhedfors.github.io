Improving Perception

Let’s take a moment for a perception activity. Look at the picture on the screen. What are your first impressions about what the man is thinking, experiencing, and feeling? Without thinking too much about them, write down all of your thoughts and impressions.

Now, look at the picture again and think about the process you just went through when developing your perception of the man.

  1. What did you notice about the man’s nonverbal communication? Looking back, did you miss any communication cues?
  2. What schema(s) did you use to understand his communication? For example, what social class did you place him in? Would your perception of him change if you found out he was a student or a CEO of a large company?
  3. Are you experiencing anything physiological that might have influenced your perception? Are you tired, hungry, etc.?
  4. Are you experiencing anything psychological that might have influenced your perception? Are you angry, scared, sad, etc.? What is your mood?
  5. Was your perception affected by any societal influences? Did you use a social bias about how men should act, how he is dressed, where he is, etc.?
