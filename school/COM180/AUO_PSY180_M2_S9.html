Self-Esteem and Competence

In the last module, you learned that competence involves being both appropriate and effective. It is about being able to accomplish your goals while also fulfilling the norms and expectations in any given situation, and maintaining your relationships. However, competence is not a black-and-white concept. Nobody demonstrates completely competent behavior all the time. Competence varies across a spectrum of appropriateness and effectiveness and depends on the individual circumstance.

Your self-esteem affects how you fulfill those norms and expectations, and it determines your effectiveness in maintaining your relationships. High self-esteem can sometimes have negative consequences, particularly because in some instances, individuals with high self-esteem may behave inappropriately to preserve their positive feelings about themselves. Low self-esteem can also affect behavior. Self-esteem can change at different times and in different situations; it is not necessarily static.
